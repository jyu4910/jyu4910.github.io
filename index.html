Hello World!

My name is Jack, and I'm currently a Business Intelligence Analyst at Sutter Health, supporting the Internal Audit department.

During undergrad, I wasn't sure what I wanted to do for my career. My older sister suggested majoring in Data Science since it was a new major at UC Berkeley at the time. I figured, why not? So, I enrolled in an intro to Computer Science course since it was a prerequisite and the professor was known as one of the best professors at UC Berkeley. However, it was tough - I had no prior programming experience, and Berkeley's CS curriculum is famously rigorous. After a challenging first semester, I considered switching majors. But before doing so, I decided to take an intro to Data Science course - and I'm glad I did. I really enjoyed the analytics side of it, which ultimately convinced me to stick with the major. I went on to earn a BA in Data Science from UC Berkeley.

After graduation, I joined Businessolver as a Product Manager Intern on the Sofia team, which manages a chatbot that answers users' benefits questions. I wrote bug and enhancement tickets, conducted basic data analysis to identify areas for improvement, and uploaded quarterly reports for clients. I was later offered a full-time role as an Associate Product Manager, where I took on additional responsibilities like managing a shared inbox, collaborating with technical teams, testing new features, and writing complex SQL queries to evaluate user engagement. Within a year, I was promoted to Product Manager Technical I.

While I enjoyed my time in product management, I found myself drawn back to data analysis. That led me to pursue analyst roles and eventually land my current position. I'm now also pursuing an MS in Analytics part-time at Georgia Tech to continue growing my skill set. Looking ahead, I'm especially interested in gaining hands-on experience in machine learning and analytics - so if you know of any opportunities, feel free to reach out at jyu663@gatech.edu!
